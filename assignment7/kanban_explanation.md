# 3. Kanban Board Explanation

### What is a Kanban Board?
A Kanban board is an interactive work visualization tool for managing projects. It's used to organize work items and monitor their transition or flow from start to finish. It serves as a main point of reference, offering a transparent, quick view of work progress and team availability.

### How the Board Functions

* **Visualizes Workflow:** Our board visualizes the workflow through clearly defined columns (Backlog, To Do, In Progress, QA, Done) that represent the lifecycle of a task. Task cards are moved from left to right as work advances, giving everyone immediate visibility into exactly where every feature or bug stands in the development pipeline.

* **Limits Work-In-Progress (WIP):** To avoid bottlenecks and reduce context-switching, our board uses WIP limits (e.g., capping the "In Progress" column to 3 active tasks per developer). This constraint ensures that the team focuses on completing current tasks before pulling new work from the "To Do" column, significantly improving cycle time and focus.

* **Supports Agile Principles:** The board intrinsically supports Agile principles such as adaptability and continuous delivery. Because the "Backlog" and "To Do" columns are fluid, we can easily reprioritize tasks as project requirements change. Furthermore, the transparent nature of the board fosters daily communication and ensures we continuously deliver tested, valuable increments through our QA and Done columns.
